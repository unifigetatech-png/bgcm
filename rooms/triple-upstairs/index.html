About the upstairs triple room

The Triple Upstairs is the same room layout as our ground-floor triple — one queen bed plus one single, sleeps three, private ensuite — but on the upper floor. Same price, same comforts. The trade-off, and the appeal, is in the floor itself. Upstairs rooms at our motel are typically the quietest. They sit further from the main check-in path, further from the pool, and further from the road below. If you sleep light, this is the room that works for you.

You will need to climb stairs to your door. We don't have a lift — and we want you to know that before booking, not after. If stairs are an issue for any reason, the same room is available on the ground floor at the same price.

Who it's for

Light sleepers go upstairs. People who've shared walls and floors with rowdy neighbours at other motels and learnt the value of being one floor up. Travelling work crews who clock off at six and want quiet by nine. Parents with one older child who appreciates a separate single bed and a quieter night. Couples plus a friend or sibling who don't mind the same room.

If you're balancing budget and quiet against an extra five steps each way, this is the right room. If those steps are a problem, our Triple Ground Floor solves that with no premium.

Why upstairs at this motel

It's worth being specific about why upstairs is the quieter choice here. Our upper floor sits above the ground-floor rooms, set back slightly from the Gold Coast Highway. Road noise is buffered by both height and the double-glazed windows on road-side rooms. The pool, reception and check-in flow all happen on the ground floor — so day-to-day movement around the property happens below you, not past your window. If you've ever been the unlucky guest in the room nearest the pool gate or the ice machine, you'll appreciate not being that guest here.

The location, in honest distances

The motel sits at 1908–1910 Gold Coast Highway in Miami, on the corner of Christine Avenue. From the front door:

  • Miami Beach — 400 metres, around 5 minutes' walk.
  • Burleigh Beach — 200m from the motel (a flat 6-minute walk).
  • Paddock Bakery — 150 metres.
  • Aldi Miami — directly across the road.
  • Burleigh Head National Park — 2.9 km, headland walk and wildlife.
  • Gold Coast Airport — 11 km, around 15 minutes' drive. Route 777 bus stops on the Gold Coast Highway nearby.

What's included in your booking

Every Triple Upstairs booking includes free use of the outdoor pool (open all year), coin-operated laundry, luggage storage at reception, and free on-site parking subject to availability — one permit per room. Daily housekeeping is included for stays of three nights or more.

Honest heads up — before you book this room:

  • Sleeps three: one queen plus one single bed. No cots, no rollaways. Children 6 and over are charged as adults.

Frequently Asked

TRIPLE UPSTAIRS — Q&A

How many stairs to the room?

One flight — a single staircase from ground level to the upper floor. We don't have a lift. If you've got mobility issues, heavy luggage, or a pram, our Triple Ground Floor is the same room layout at the same $165/night without the stairs.

How is the upstairs different from the ground floor triple?

Same room layout, same bedding (queen plus single), same private ensuite, same price. The only meaningful difference is the floor and what comes with it.

Upstairs is typically quieter — set back from the road, above the ground-floor traffic, with double-glazed windows on road-facing rooms. Ground floor is easier — no stairs, walk straight in from the car park. Light sleepers tend to prefer upstairs; older guests, families with prams, or anyone with knees that don't love stairs tend to prefer ground.

Are the upstairs rooms quieter than the ground floor?

Yes, in our experience. The upper floor sits above the day-to-day movement around the motel — pool, reception, check-in flow all happen on the ground floor. Road noise is buffered by height and by the double-glazed windows on road-facing rooms.

How many people can sleep here?

Three. One queen plus one single. Two adults and one child, three friends, or a parent with one or two kids over 6. No cots, no rollaways.

Are there double-glazed windows for noise?

All road-facing rooms have double-glazed windows for noise and heat insulation. With Light Rail Stage 3 works happening nearby until July 2026, we recommend road-facing rooms either way — the glazing genuinely makes a difference. Garden-facing rooms don't need it; they're already quiet.

Is parking included?

Yes — free on-site parking, one permit per room, subject to availability. Earlier arrivals get the better odds. Street parking is also available outside the motel on Christine Avenue.

Can I book the upstairs triple directly?

Yes — and it's the cheapest way. Book through our website or call (07) 5576 3211. No platform fees, no resort fees. We'll match any lower public rate.
