Why stay 25 minutes away

Sea World is at Main Beach — the closest of the three big Gold Coast theme parks to the motel. It's about 25 minutes north up the Gold Coast Highway through Surfers Paradise, then across the Sundale Bridge. Park-side hotels around the Marina Mirage strip charge $300+ a night in season; we start at $125 with free parking, no booking fees and no resort fees. On a three-night family trip the saving usually covers a sit-down dinner and an extra ride.

The drive itself is the easiest of the three because you don't need the M1. Head straight up the Gold Coast Highway — it goes past Mermaid, Broadbeach and Surfers, then becomes Sea World Drive after the bridge. If the Surfers strip is heavy, the alternate is M1 to Exit 71 (Smith Street Motorway), which is sometimes faster on weekends.

The day, honestly

Sea World is the most family-friendly of the three big parks. The thrill rides are lighter than Movie World's, the marine animals carry the day for under-7s, and the Sea Life aquarium gives you a quiet indoor break when the heat hits 30+ degrees. Plan a full day — gates open at 10am and most families use every hour to closing at 5pm.

The headline sections are Sea Life Aquarium with an underwater ocean tunnel, the Penguin Encounter, Shark Bay, the Dolphin Lagoon, and the Nickelodeon Land rides themed around SpongeBob, Paw Patrol and the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. Animal feeding times and dolphin shows run on a published daily schedule — grab the program at the gate and build your day around the ones your kids care about.

If you're chasing genuine thrills, the standout is the Storm Coaster — a water-based coaster that combines the splash of a flume ride with proper drops. The Jet Rescue coaster is good fun and fits younger riders thanks to its lower height limit.

Tickets and value

Buy tickets from the official Village Roadshow Theme Parks website rather than the gate — gate prices are noticeably higher. The multi-park Mega Pass covers Sea World, Movie World and Wet'n'Wild on unlimited entry for around the price of two single-day tickets. If you're doing two or more parks across the trip, it's almost always the smart move. Annual passes pay for themselves on a three-day visit.

We don't sell tickets at reception. Buy direct from the park and the price you see online is the price you pay.

What to bring

  • Sunscreen and a hat. Most of Sea World is open-air; the aquarium is the only consistently shaded section.
  • A reusable water bottle. There are refill stations throughout. Bottled water inside is around $5.
  • A change of clothes if the kids are doing the Storm Coaster or splash pads — they will get wet.
  • Snacks for the morning. Park food is fine but pricey, especially for kids who pick at it.
  • The motel pool at the end of the day — you'll want it before dinner.
Honest heads up: Sea World's animal exhibits are popular for good reason, but the dolphin show and seal presentations are timed and short. Don't show up at the lagoon at 4:55pm expecting one — check the program in the morning and plan from there.

Frequently Asked

SEA WORLD — Q&A

How long does it take to drive from the motel to Sea World?

Around 25 minutes off-peak — straight north up the Gold Coast Highway through Surfers Paradise, then across the Sundale Bridge to Main Beach.

School holidays and Saturdays add 10–15 minutes through the Surfers strip. The alternate route is M1 north to Exit 71 (Smith Street Motorway), which is sometimes faster on weekends.

Is Sea World suitable for very young kids?

Yes — arguably the best of the three big parks for under-7s. The Nickelodeon Land rides have low height limits, the marine animals carry the day, the Sea Life aquarium tunnels work for any age, and the touch pools and penguin encounters are favourites.

The thrill rides (Storm Coaster, Jet Rescue) are lighter than Movie World's headliners.

Do we need a full day at Sea World?

Yes — plan a full day. Between the marine animals, Sea Life aquarium, Nickelodeon rides and the scheduled shows and feedings, most families use every hour from 10am opening to 5pm closing.

Animal feeding times and dolphin presentations run on a published daily schedule — grab the program at the gate and build your day around the ones the kids care about.

Is parking free at Sea World?

Yes — Sea World has a large free general car park. The car park is right next to the entrance, so you don't need to budget for a long walk in.

Parking at the motel is also free for guests, one permit per room subject to availability.

Should we buy Sea World tickets at the gate or online?

Online, every time. Single-day tickets, multi-park Mega Passes (Sea World + Movie World + Wet'n'Wild) and annual passes are noticeably cheaper on the official Village Roadshow Theme Parks website than at the gate.

We don't sell tickets at reception.

Can we visit Sea Life aquarium without buying a Sea World ticket?

No — Sea Life is included in your Sea World admission, but you can't enter just for the aquarium. The Sea Life Sunshine Coast (further north, separate park) is a standalone aquarium, but Sea World's is wrapped into the main park.
