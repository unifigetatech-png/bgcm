Why stay 30 minutes away

Wet'n'Wild sits at Oxenford, immediately next to Movie World, just off the Pacific Motorway about 30 minutes north of the motel. Park-side resorts charge $300 a night and up in season — and you'll arrive home to a view of a car park, not a pool. We start at $125 with free parking at both ends, no resort fees and no booking fees. After a long day in the water, our outdoor pool, the 5-minute walk to Miami Beach and the easy run to Burleigh's cafés is a much better landing.

The drive is the same as Movie World: north on the Gold Coast Highway, onto the M1 at Burleigh, exit at 60 (Oxenford / Helensvale). Off-peak it's around 30 minutes. School holidays and Saturdays add 10–20, so leaving by 8:30am puts you at the gates near opening.

The day, honestly

Wet'n'Wild is the biggest water park in Australia, and you'll genuinely feel that walking around it. Plan a full day — opening is usually 10am with last entry around 3pm and the park closing 4–5pm depending on the season. There's a reliable pattern that works: do the queue-heavy speed slides early before the lines build, eat lunch out of the sun around 1pm, and then float in the lazy river, paddle in the wave pool or chuck the kids in Buccaneer Bay for the afternoon.

The headline rides are the Kamikaze double-drop slide, the Aqualoop with its trapdoor launch, the Tornado family raft ride, and the Black Hole tube slide. The big wave pool runs on a 20-minute cycle and gets proper waves — bigger than they look from the deck. The lazy river is exactly what you want at 2pm, and Buccaneer Bay is the dedicated kids' play structure with smaller slides, water cannons and shallow pools.

Food is cafeteria-style and fine, but pricey for a family of four. Most regulars bring snacks — there's an esky drop at the entrance for cold storage during the day.

Tickets and value

Buy tickets from the official Village Roadshow Theme Parks website — gate prices are noticeably higher. The multi-park Mega Pass covers Wet'n'Wild, Movie World and Sea World on unlimited entry for around the price of two single-day tickets, which makes it the easy choice if you're doing more than one park. Annual passes pay for themselves on a three-day visit.

We don't sell tickets at reception. Buy direct from the park.

What to bring

  • Your own towel. Hire towels are available at the park but bringing your own saves $10–$15.
  • A waterproof phone pouch on a lanyard. The lockers work but pouches are easier between rides.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen. Reapply every two hours. The water washes it off faster than you think.
  • A rashie for kids — they'll be in the sun all day.
  • Cash for lockers if you don't bring a pouch. Lockers run $15–$20 for the day.
  • A change of dry clothes for the drive home. Wet swimmers in air conditioning is misery.
Honest heads up: Wet'n'Wild is best in the warmer months. The park does close some attractions in winter (May–September). If you're travelling outside summer, check the official park calendar before you book — it's still a great day, just not the full ride list.

Frequently Asked

WET 'N' WILD — Q&A

How long does it take to drive from the motel to Wet'n'Wild?

Around 30 minutes off-peak — straight up the M1 from the motel to Exit 60 (Oxenford / Helensvale). The exit is shared with Movie World; Wet'n'Wild is right next door.

School holidays and Saturdays add 10–20 minutes. Leaving by 8:30am puts you at the gates near opening.

What's the best month to visit Wet'n'Wild?

October through April is the peak season — water temperatures are most comfortable, all rides are open, and the days are long enough to make a full day worth it.

The park does close some attractions in winter (May–September). If you're travelling outside summer, check the official park calendar before you book.

Can young kids enjoy Wet'n'Wild?

Yes — there's a dedicated Buccaneer Bay kids' area with shallow pools, gentle slides, water cannons and shade. The lazy river works for any age. The big speed slides and Aqualoop have firm height limits, usually 110–120cm.

If your kids fall short of the big-slide heights, the day still works — Buccaneer Bay alone can fill an afternoon.

Do they hire towels and lockers?

Yes — towels can be hired and lockers rented at the park. Bringing your own towel saves $10–$15. Lockers are usually $15–$20 for the day.

A waterproof phone pouch on a lanyard is the most practical alternative to constantly returning to a locker between rides.

Can we do Wet'n'Wild and Movie World on the same trip?

Yes — they share the same M1 exit and effectively the same car park. Most people split them across two days; the multi-park Mega Pass from Village Roadshow makes this cheap and unlimited entry across both.

Doing both on one day is possible but exhausting. If you do try it, water park in the morning when the queues are short, theme park in the afternoon for the shows.

Is parking free at Wet'n'Wild?

Yes — Wet'n'Wild has a large free general car park shared with Movie World. There are paid premium options closer to the entrance, but standard parking is fine for most visitors.

Parking at the motel is also free for guests, one permit per room subject to availability.
