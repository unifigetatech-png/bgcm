1. Who we are

"Burleigh Gold Coast Motel" (also "we", "us", "our") operates the website at www.burleighmotel.com.au and the motel located at 1908–1910 Gold Coast Highway, Miami QLD 4220. We are bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles.

This policy explains what personal information we collect about you, why, what we do with it, and the choices you have. ABN available on request.

2. What information we collect

The personal information we collect depends on how you interact with us. It typically includes:

  • Booking and stay information: name, email, phone number, postal address, dates of stay, room preferences, vehicle registration, ID details collected at check-in, and any special requests you give us.
  • Payment information: credit card details handled through our secure payment provider. We do not store full card numbers on our own systems.
  • Communications: emails, phone calls, voicemails and messages you send us.
  • Website usage: IP address, device and browser information, pages viewed and interactions, captured by analytics cookies (see Section 5).
  • CCTV: common areas of the motel (reception, car park, exterior) are monitored by CCTV for the safety and security of guests and staff. Signage at the property identifies the camera locations.

You can usually choose not to give us some information — but it may limit our ability to make a booking, process a payment or assist you.

3. How we use your information

We use your personal information to:

  • Process and confirm your booking, take payment, and provide the accommodation and services you've asked for.
  • Communicate with you about your booking, arrival, stay, and post-stay matters (receipts, invoices, follow-up).
  • Comply with our legal obligations — including guest registration, tax invoicing, and law enforcement requests where required.
  • Keep our website and motel running, secure and reliable.
  • Improve our services, including by reviewing booking patterns and aggregated website analytics.
  • Send you marketing communications about the motel — only where you've consented or where the law allows it. You can unsubscribe at any time.

4. Who we share information with

We share personal information only where necessary to operate our business, and only with parties bound to keep it confidential:

  • Our booking and property management system (Newbook), which processes reservations and payments.
  • Payment processors and banks for the purpose of taking and reconciling payments.
  • Service providers we use to operate the website and motel (hosting, email, analytics, accounting, security).
  • Government authorities when required by law — for example, in response to a valid subpoena, court order, or police request.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share it for third-party marketing purposes.

Some of our service providers may store information overseas. Where this happens, we take reasonable steps to ensure the recipient handles your information consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles.

5. Cookies, analytics and embedded content

This website uses cookies and similar technologies to make the site work, remember your preferences, and understand how visitors use it.

Cookies we set or rely on

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for booking, security and basic site functionality.
  • Analytics cookies (Google Analytics 4) — measure aggregate visitor behaviour so we can improve the site. We do not use this data to identify individual visitors.
  • Embedded content cookies — set by Google Maps and our embedded booking iframe (Newbook). Their use is governed by their own privacy policies.

You can disable cookies in your browser settings, but parts of the site (especially the booking flow) may not work properly without them.

6. How long we keep your information

We keep personal information only as long as we need it for the purposes set out above, or as required by law (including taxation and corporate records, generally for at least seven years). When we no longer need your information, we delete or de-identify it securely.

7. Your rights

Under the Privacy Act, you have the right to:

  • Ask what personal information we hold about you.
  • Ask us to correct information that is inaccurate, out of date or incomplete.
  • Ask us to delete information we no longer need (subject to our legal record-keeping obligations).
  • Withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time, by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any email or contacting us directly.
  • Make a complaint about how we've handled your information.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 9. We may need to verify your identity before acting on your request. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your information from misuse, loss, unauthorised access, modification and disclosure. These include encrypted website connections (HTTPS), restricted staff access, secure payment processing and a clean-desk policy at reception. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of the page shows when it was last changed. If we make material changes that affect how we use your personal information, we'll let you know prominently on this site.
