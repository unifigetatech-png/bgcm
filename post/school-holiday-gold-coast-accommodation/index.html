Why Burleigh Heads Makes the Right Family Base

The Gold Coast is a 57km stretch of coast in Queensland, Australia — south of Brisbane, north of the New South Wales border. It has four major theme parks (Movie World, Sea World, Wet 'n' Wild, and Dreamworld), dozens of kilometres of patrolled beach, national parks, water parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and an enormous amount of free space for families who just want to be outside.

Burleigh Heads sits in the middle of the southern half of the Gold Coast — far enough from Surfers Paradise to avoid the tourist congestion, close enough to theme parks that no drive is longer than 32 minutes. It has its own national park (free), its own patrolled beach (free), a shallow creek swimming spot at Tallebudgera (free), foreshore parks with free BBQs, and a café and restaurant strip on James Street. That combination of free activities around a good beach is what makes Burleigh the smart family base.

The Theme Parks — What to Expect and When to Go

All four major Gold Coast theme parks are within 32 minutes of the motel. Entry tickets are sold individually per-park or as multi-park passes — buying a Village Roadshow pass (covering Movie World, Sea World and Wet 'n' Wild) typically cuts the per-day cost compared to single-day entry.

Free Activities for Kids

Theme parks are the headline, but a Gold Coast school holiday that only goes park-to-park gets expensive quickly. The free activities fill the days between park visits and, for younger kids especially, they are often the days remembered most.

Practical Tips for Families on the Gold Coast

Book theme park tickets online in advance. Gate prices are higher. Multi-park Village Roadshow passes (covering Movie World, Sea World and Wet 'n' Wild) cut the per-day cost significantly over two or three parks.

Go early on park days. Theme parks are significantly quieter in the first two hours after opening. The queues at Movie World at 9:30am are a fraction of what they are at 11:30am. Arriving early and leaving mid-afternoon also avoids the late-afternoon crash when kids are tired.

Alternate park days with beach or creek days. Back-to-back theme parks is expensive and exhausting. Tallebudgera Creek, John Laws Park, and Burleigh Beach are all free and often more memorable for younger kids. The motel pool is also a legitimate all-afternoon option on a hot day.

Use the bar fridge and microwave. Every room has one. Breakfast from the room instead of a café saves $25–$40 per morning for a family of four.

Book early for school holidays. Family-sized rooms fill first. Winter school holidays (July) and the Christmas/January period fill weeks or months in advance at most accommodation on the Gold Coast.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common questions about Gold Coast school holiday accommodation for families.

Where is the best Gold Coast accommodation for families with kids?
Burleigh Heads and Miami offer the best balance for families: close to all major theme parks (26–32 min), close to patrolled beaches, with family-sized motel rooms significantly cheaper than beachfront resort rooms. Burleigh Gold Coast Motel has family rooms (sleeps 5) from $195/night and interconnecting rooms (sleeps 6+) from $295/night.
How far is Burleigh Gold Coast Motel from Movie World?
Movie World is approximately 25km — a 26-minute drive via the M1. Sea World is 29km (29 min). Wet 'n' Wild is 25km (26 min). Dreamworld is 29km (29 min).
Are there family-sized rooms at Burleigh Gold Coast Motel?
Yes — the Family Room has a queen bed plus three singles and sleeps up to 5 (from $195/night). For larger families, Interconnecting Rooms offer two connected rooms with two private ensuites, sleeping 6 or more (from $295/night).
What free activities are there for kids in Burleigh?
Burleigh Beach and Miami Beach are both patrolled and free. John Laws Park has free gas BBQs on the foreshore. Tallebudgera Creek is shallow, warm-water swimming 2km south (free). The Burleigh Heads National Park walk is free and suits school-age kids.
When is the best time to visit the Gold Coast for school holidays?
Queensland school holidays fall in April, July, September and December. July and September offer warm-to-mild weather. December/January is peak summer — hot and very busy. Book early for all school holiday periods; family-sized rooms fill fast.
