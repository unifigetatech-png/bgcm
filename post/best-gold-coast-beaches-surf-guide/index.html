The Gold Coast — Beach by Beach

The Gold Coast runs north-south along the Pacific Ocean in Queensland, Australia — about 80 kilometres south of Brisbane. The coast faces east, which means mornings are typically calmer than afternoons when the sea breeze picks up. Water temperature ranges from around 19°C in winter (July–August) to 25°C or higher in summer (January–February). All major beaches are patrolled by surf lifesavers during peak periods — swimming between the flags applies here the same as it does on any Australian beach.

Every beach on this list can be reached from Burleigh Gold Coast Motel in 20 minutes or under, which is one of the practical arguments for choosing Burleigh as your base. The motel sits centrally enough on the strip that you are not committing to one end of the coast or the other.

Burleigh Heads — Best Overall

★ Best Overall Surf: Intermediate–Advanced Swimming: Good Family-Friendly

Burleigh Beach runs south-facing and wide in front of the headland, catching swell from the south and east. The break at the point is a right-hander that walls up from the rocks and runs for a considerable distance — one of the most-watched waves on the east coast. On solid south swells, it is genuinely outstanding. On smaller days, it is consistent enough to be worth paddling out even for intermediate surfers with some experience.

The beach itself is patrolled and safe for swimming away from the point. Behind it, Burleigh Heads National Park starts where the sand ends — a 45-minute headland loop through subtropical rainforest with clifftop ocean views. It is the only beach on the Gold Coast where you can surf, swim, and walk through a national park without getting in a car between them.

Distance from motel: 200m walk (6 minutes). No driving required.

Kirra Beach — Best Surf

Surf: Advanced Swimming: Moderate

Kirra is at the southern end of the Gold Coast, in the suburb of Coolangatta near the New South Wales border. On its best days — a solid south or south-southeast groundswell — it produces one of the longest and fastest right-hand point breaks in the world. The wave peels down a sand-covered rock shelf at speed, with sections that barrel cleanly and a ride length that can stretch to several hundred metres when conditions align.

For non-surfers, Kirra has a long patrolled beach with calm swimming conditions on smaller days, a surf club, and good views of the point from the park above. The Coolangatta precinct nearby has coffee, food and the kind of laid-back southern Gold Coast atmosphere that is distinctly different from the commercial strip north of Broadbeach.

Distance from motel: 16km south, approximately 15 minutes by car on the Gold Coast Highway.

Broadbeach — Best Facilities

Swimming: Excellent Family-Friendly

Broadbeach is 6km north of the motel — about 8 minutes by car. The beach here is wide and well-sheltered, with consistent surf lifesaving patrol coverage and a long, flat wave that works for beginner surfers. The main draw is the precinct behind the beach: Pacific Fair shopping centre (the largest in Queensland), The Star Gold Coast casino, HOTA (Home of the Arts), and a concentrated strip of restaurants and bars that makes it a strong evening destination.

Distance from motel: 6km north, 8 minutes by car. The light rail (tram) also connects Broadbeach to the northern suburbs, with a stop at Broadbeach South walking distance from the beach.

North Burleigh — Best for Families

Swimming: Excellent ★ Best for Families

North Burleigh Beach is the stretch immediately in front of the motel — a 5-minute walk from the front entrance, 400m north of Burleigh Beach itself. The beach here is wide, patrolled, and faces north-east, meaning it catches less south swell than Burleigh proper and has a flatter, safer shoreline for children and inexperienced swimmers.

John Laws Park runs directly behind the beach with a playground, BBQ facilities, covered picnic shelters, and grass areas that stay shaded in the morning. The foreshore path connects north toward Broadbeach and south toward the headland. It is the most practical day-to-day beach for motel guests, particularly families who want to walk there without driving.

Tallebudgera Creek — Best for Young Kids

★ Best for Young Children Sheltered Freshwater

Tallebudgera Creek is not technically a beach — it is a tidal lagoon where a freshwater creek meets the ocean through a broad sandbar, 2km south of the motel. The water is warm, shallow and sheltered. There is no surf. You can wade across the sandbar at low tide. It is consistently the most recommended spot on the Gold Coast for families with young children who are not yet confident in open surf.

Free parking is available on the south side. There are picnic tables and BBQs along the creek bank, and the water temperature here runs 2–3°C warmer than the open ocean due to the sand-filtered freshwater mixing. Distance from motel: 2km south, 4-minute drive or a 25-minute walk.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

The most common questions about Gold Coast beaches and surfing.

What is the best surfing beach on the Gold Coast?
Kirra Beach at Coolangatta is regarded as one of the best point breaks in the world on its day — a long right-hand wave wrapping around a shallow sand point. Burleigh Heads is the other major point break on the Gold Coast, better suited to intermediate surfers and more consistent day to day. Snapper Rocks at Coolangatta hosts the annual WSL event and is one of the three-break stretch (Snapper, Greenmount, Kirra) on solid south swells.
Which Gold Coast beach is best for families?
North Burleigh Beach (patrolled, flat water, park with BBQs and playground directly behind) and Tallebudgera Creek (warm, shallow tidal lagoon — the best for young children) are the top choices. Broadbeach is also family-friendly with good lifesaving patrol coverage and facilities directly behind the beach.
Is Burleigh Heads beach good for swimming?
Yes. Burleigh Beach is patrolled by surf lifesavers during peak hours. Swimming between the flags is safe. The surf break is at the southern headland — the main beach is calm enough for confident swimmers. Tallebudgera Creek, 2km south, is the better option for young children or non-swimmers.
How far is Kirra Beach from Burleigh Gold Coast Motel?
Kirra Beach is approximately 16km south of the motel — about a 15-minute drive on the Gold Coast Highway. It is in the Coolangatta suburb, near the New South Wales border and Gold Coast Airport.
What is the Gold Coast water temperature?
Gold Coast ocean water temperatures range from around 19–20°C in July–August (cool but swimmable) to 24–26°C in January–March. The coast faces east, so mornings are generally calmer than afternoons. Tallebudgera Creek typically runs 2–3°C warmer than the open ocean.
