The Problem with Park Hotels

Theme park resorts charge $300+ per night. Add $30 parking. Add overpriced resort food. A three-night family trip costs hundreds more than it needs to.

The parks are a day out — not a 24/7 experience. You arrive, you ride, you leave. So why pay resort prices for a bed you only use to sleep?

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Everything theme-park families ask before booking.

How far is Burleigh Gold Coast Motel from the theme parks?
Movie World and Wet 'n' Wild are 26 minutes north on the M1. Sea World is 29 minutes. Dreamworld is 32 minutes. Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary is just 10 minutes south down the Gold Coast Highway.
How much do you save by staying here instead of at a park resort?
Roughly $200 per night. Park-front hotels start around $300/night with $30/night parking on top. Our rooms start at $125/night with free parking. Over three nights, that's $600+ back in your pocket — about two extra park tickets.
Which room is best for a family of four?
The Quad Room — queen bed plus two singles, sleeps four — from $185/night. For five, the Family Room sleeps five from $195/night. Larger groups, the Interconnecting handles 6+ with two rooms and two bathrooms.
Is there parking? How much?
Free on-site parking, one permit per room, subject to availability. Big enough for utes, vans and trailers — handy if you're doing the parks for the day.
Can you walk to the beach?
Miami Beach is a 5-minute walk (400m). Burleigh Beach with its iconic headland and national park trail is 200m down the highway — perfect for a sunset walk after a long park day.
Is the motel suitable for early or late check-in around theme park hours?
Reception is 9am–7:30pm daily. Park gates usually open at 9:30am, so check-in works around your park day. If you're arriving late, call us — we'll arrange after-hours collection.
