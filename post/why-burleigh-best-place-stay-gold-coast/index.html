The Gold Coast is a coastal city in Queensland, Australia — about 80 kilometres south of Brisbane, bordering New South Wales to the south. Most international visitors arrive via Brisbane Airport (roughly an hour north on the M1 motorway) or Gold Coast Airport at Coolangatta (20 minutes south of the motel). The coast runs about 60 kilometres north to south along the Pacific Ocean. Surfers Paradise is the busy commercial heart. Coolangatta is the relaxed southern end. Burleigh Heads sits between them, and the position turns out to be close to ideal.

The Beach is Different Here

Burleigh Beach runs wide and south-facing in front of the headland, angled in a way that catches the swell cleanly. It is not the long tourist-packed stretch of sand you find in Surfers Paradise. On any given morning, locals park on the foreshore, grab a coffee from James Street, and watch the surf for half an hour before work. It is that kind of beach.

The surf break at the headland is a point break — one of the better-known waves on the east coast of Australia. Waves wrap around the rocky point in long, clean lines. Even if you have never surfed, it is worth watching. The flat rocks above the break are one of the better free seats on the Gold Coast.

A National Park at the End of the Sand

At the southern end of Burleigh Beach, the sand runs straight into Burleigh Heads National Park. No gate, no entry fee — the walking track just begins where the beach ends. The loop around the headland takes about 45 minutes through subtropical rainforest, along clifftop platforms and past ocean lookouts that are genuinely hard to leave.

This is the thing that separates Burleigh from every other suburb on the Gold Coast. You can walk from your motel room into a national park within 10 minutes. No other part of the strip offers that. The park has pandanus palms, strangler figs, rainbow lorikeets, and views back toward the city skyline that put most observation decks to shame.

The Food Scene Burleigh Built

James Street, running parallel to the beach through central Burleigh, has become one of the most talked-about short strips of food and coffee in southeast Queensland. Specialty roasters, juice bars, wine bars and restaurants that fill their bookings weeks ahead. It is the kind of street that makes you extend your stay by a day just to get back to the same table.

Walking it in the morning costs nothing. Eating well costs less than you would spend at a resort breakfast. The food scene here is one of the main reasons people who visit Burleigh for a weekend end up coming back three or four times.

Close to Everything That Matters

The practical case for Burleigh is just as strong as the lifestyle case. Every major Gold Coast theme park — Movie World, Wet 'n' Wild, Dreamworld, Sea World — is 26–32 minutes north on the M1 motorway. Gold Coast Airport at Coolangatta is 20 minutes south. Brisbane's international airport is just over an hour north. Broadbeach, Pacific Fair shopping centre, and HOTA (Home of the Arts) are 8 minutes away.

You can do a theme park day, get back by 4pm, walk to the headland for sunset and be at a James Street restaurant by 7pm. That is a full day that costs nothing extra in travel time. That is the case for Burleigh as your base.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What travellers ask most often before choosing Burleigh as their Gold Coast base.

Where exactly is Burleigh Heads on the Gold Coast?
Burleigh Heads is on the southern half of the Gold Coast — about 10km south of Surfers Paradise and 22km north of Gold Coast Airport at Coolangatta. It sits between the busiest commercial part of the strip and the quiet southern end, close to everything without being in the middle of the noise.
Is Burleigh Heads better than Surfers Paradise for a holiday?
For most travellers, yes. Burleigh has a better beach, a national park walk, a respected café and food scene on James Street, and is noticeably quieter. It lacks the nightclub strip of Surfers, which most families and couples consider an advantage rather than a downside.
How far is Burleigh Heads from Gold Coast Airport?
Gold Coast Airport at Coolangatta is about 22km south of the motel — roughly a 20-minute drive on the Gold Coast Highway. Brisbane Airport is approximately 90km north, around an hour on the M1 motorway.
What is Burleigh Heads known for?
Burleigh is known for its headland national park walk, its point break surf (one of the most-photographed on the east coast), James Street's café and restaurant scene, and Tallebudgera Creek — a warm, shallow freshwater swimming spot 2km south. It is also home to the Bleach* Festival and the Burleigh Heads surf club.
Is Burleigh a good base for visiting the Gold Coast theme parks?
Yes. Movie World, Wet 'n' Wild and Dreamworld are all 26–32 minutes north on the M1. Sea World is 29 minutes north. Currumbin Wildlife Sanctuary is just 10 minutes south. All parks are an easy morning drive, and you save $150+ per night compared to staying at a park-adjacent resort.
How far is Burleigh Gold Coast Motel from the beach?
Miami Beach is a 5-minute walk (400m). The motel is 200m from Burleigh Beach (a flat 6-minute walk). Both beaches are patrolled by surf lifesavers during peak hours.
