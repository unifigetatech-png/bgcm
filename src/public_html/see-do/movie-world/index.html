Why stay 30 minutes away

Movie World sits at Oxenford, just off the Pacific Motorway about 30 minutes north of the motel. Park-side resorts charge $300 a night and up in peak season. We start at $125, with free parking at both ends, no resort fees and no booking fees. On a three-night Gold Coast trip that's typically $400–$500 back in your pocket — usually enough to cover an extra park ticket or the dinner the kids actually want.

The drive is straightforward. Head north on the Gold Coast Highway, jump on the M1 at Burleigh, and get off at Exit 60 (Oxenford / Helensvale). From the motel forecourt to the Movie World gates is around 30 minutes off-peak. School holidays and Saturday mornings add 10–20 minutes, so leaving by 8:30am gets you there near opening.

The day, honestly

Movie World is the biggest single-park day on the Gold Coast. Plan a full day — gates open at 9:30am, and most families burn through every hour to closing at 5:00pm. There's no way to do it justice in a half day, especially if anyone in your group is tall enough for the big coasters.

The headline rides are DC Rivals HyperCoaster, Superman Escape, the Green Lantern coaster, and Doomsday Destroyer. Younger kids stick to Looney Tunes Village, the Justice League dark ride and Scooby-Doo Spooky Coaster, all of which have lower height limits. There's a daily Hollywood stunt-driver show and rolling character meet-and-greets with Bugs Bunny, Wonder Woman, Batman and the Looney Tunes crew.

Food inside the park is theme-park food at theme-park prices. If you'd rather skip that, you can leave the park, eat back near the highway at one of the pubs or food courts in Oxenford, and re-enter the same day with your wristband.

Tickets and value

Buy tickets from the official Village Roadshow Theme Parks website rather than at the gate — gate prices are noticeably higher. The multi-park Mega Pass covers Movie World, Sea World and Wet'n'Wild on unlimited entry for around the price of two single-day tickets, so if you're doing more than one park it's almost always the smart move. Annual passes pay for themselves on a four-day trip.

We don't sell tickets at reception. We don't take commissions on park passes. This is just what works for guests.

What to bring

  • Sunscreen and a hat. The queues for the big coasters are partially shaded, but the rest of the park bakes from 11am.
  • A reusable water bottle. There are refill stations throughout. You'll save $5 a person, every time you drink.
  • Phone in a clip pouch. Loose pockets and inversion coasters don't mix.
  • A change of shoes. By 3pm everyone wants their thongs.
  • Cash for the car park machine if you're paying for premium parking. Standard parking is free.
Honest heads up: Movie World's biggest coasters have firm height limits and 30-minute waits during peak. If you're travelling with under-7s, the day still works — but plan it around their rides, not yours. The kids' areas are genuinely good.

Frequently Asked

MOVIE WORLD — Q&A

How long does it take to drive from the motel to Movie World?

Around 30 minutes off-peak — straight up the M1 from the motel to Exit 60 (Oxenford / Helensvale). Allow an extra 10–20 minutes during school holidays, weekends, and from 8:30am onwards on a typical morning.

If you want to be at the gates near opening, leave the motel by 8:30am on a holiday and 8:50am on a quieter day.

Is parking free at Movie World?

Yes — Movie World has a large free general car park. There are paid premium parking options closer to the entrance, but the standard free parking is fine for most visitors and adds a maybe-5-minute walk.

Parking at the motel is also free for guests, one permit per room subject to availability.

How much do we save staying at Burleigh vs the park-side hotels?

Park-side resorts typically run $300+ per night in peak season. Our Double Room starts at $125 and our Quad starts at $185. Over a three-night stay that's $400 to $500 back — and there are no resort fees, no booking fees and no parking fees.

Trade-off: 30 minutes' drive each way. Many families decide that's worth a meal out.

Should we buy Movie World tickets at the gate or online?

Online, every time. Single-day, multi-park (Movie World + Sea World + Wet'n'Wild) and annual passes are noticeably cheaper on the official Village Roadshow Theme Parks website than at the gate.

We don't sell tickets at reception — buy direct from the park.

Can we do Movie World and Wet'n'Wild on the same trip?

Yes — they share the same M1 exit and effectively the same car park area at Oxenford. Most people split them across two separate days; the multi-park Mega Pass from Village Roadshow makes this cheap.

If you're trying to do both on one day, focus on Movie World in the morning and the lazy-river end of Wet'n'Wild in the afternoon when you're already worn out.

Are there any rides for very young kids?

Yes — Looney Tunes Village is built for kids under 8 with a roller coaster, a carousel, and several walk-around character meets. Justice League: Alien Invasion 3D is a gentle dark ride that most kids handle.

The big drop coasters (DC Rivals, Superman Escape, Green Lantern) all have firm height limits — usually 130–140cm. If your kids fall short, plan the day around the kids' rides and let one adult tag-out for the bigger coasters.
