About the Interconnecting Rooms

Interconnecting Rooms are two of our standard rooms — adjoining, with a connecting interior door between them — booked together as one stay. You get the privacy of two separate rooms with the convenience of moving between them through the inside door rather than the corridor. Each side has its own private ensuite, its own air conditioning, its own flat-screen TV, its own fridge and kettle. Free Wi-Fi works across both rooms. It's the closest thing we have to a family suite at the Burleigh Gold Coast Motel.

Both rooms are upstairs — stairs only, no lift. From $295 a night for the pair, sleeps up to six depending on bed configuration. Bed setup can vary depending on which rooms are interconnected on your dates, so we recommend calling reception on (07) 5576 3211 before booking if you have a specific configuration in mind.

Who it's for

Larger families who've outgrown the Family Room — five or six people who want kids in one room and parents in the other, with a door between them rather than down the hall. Multi-generation stays where grandparents are travelling with the family and want their own space without booking on a different floor. Two couples travelling together who want their own ensuites and their own privacy, but to be next door for breakfast and beach trips.

The connecting door is the feature that makes this different from "two rooms next to each other". You can pop into the kids' room at 11pm to check on them without standing in the corridor in your dressing gown. You can leave the door open during the day so the families flow between rooms naturally. You can close it at bedtime and have full privacy. It's the small detail that makes the price difference worthwhile for the right group.

Why two ensuites matters

The single biggest practical advantage of the Interconnecting Rooms over the Family Room or the Quad is the second bathroom. With six people getting ready for a beach day at 9am, the difference between one ensuite and two ensuites is the difference between fifty minutes of bathroom queue and twenty minutes. With teenagers, that maths gets even more painful with one bathroom. The Interconnecting Rooms turn the morning rush into a non-event for larger groups.

Frequently Asked

INTERCONNECTING ROOM — Q&A

What does "interconnecting" actually mean?

Two of our standard motel rooms, side by side, with a connecting door inside the rooms — so you can move between them without going out into the corridor. Each room has its own external door too, but the interior connecting door is what makes the booking work for families and groups.

How many beds, and what's the configuration?

Bed setup depends on which two rooms are connected on your dates. Common configurations are queen + queen, queen + queen-and-single, or queen + queen-and-two-singles. Sleeps up to six.

If a specific bed setup matters — e.g. you need two queens for two couples, or a queen and three singles for parents and kids — please call reception on (07) 5576 3211 before booking. We'll confirm what's actually available on your dates rather than promising a configuration we can't deliver.

Are there really two bathrooms?

Yes — one private ensuite per room, so two ensuites total. This is the main reason families and larger groups pick the Interconnecting Rooms over the Family Room. With six people, two bathrooms changes the morning rush significantly.

Can I lock the connecting door?

Yes — the connecting door locks from both sides. So during the day with kids, you can leave it open and let the rooms feel like one space. At night or when one half wants privacy, lock it from inside.

Is this cheaper than booking two separate rooms?

It's priced as a package — $300 a night for the pair. That works out cheaper than two separate $165 triples ($330) and similar to a Quad plus a Double. The real value isn't the dollar saving, though — it's the connecting door. You can't get that by booking two unrelated rooms.

Are both rooms upstairs?

Yes — both rooms are on the upper floor and reached by stairs. We don't have a lift. If stairs are an issue for any of your group, please call before booking. We don't have an interconnecting setup on the ground floor.

Do we get two parking permits?

Yes — one free permit per room, so two for the interconnecting booking. Both subject to availability on the day. Free street parking is also available outside the motel if you have a third vehicle.

Can I book the Interconnecting Rooms directly?

Yes. Book through our website or call (07) 5576 3211. For Interconnecting Rooms specifically, calling first is a good idea — we can confirm bed configuration, parking, and that both halves are available on your exact dates before you commit.
