Location — What the Address Actually Means

The motel's address is 1908–1910 Gold Coast Highway, Corner Christine Avenue, Miami QLD 4220. Miami is technically a separate suburb from Burleigh Heads — one suburb to the north — but most visitors and many locals refer to the stretch from Miami to Burleigh as one area. The motel is 200m from Burleigh Beach (a flat 6-minute walk). James Street, the national park, and the headland are a short walk beyond the patrolled beach.

For context, the Gold Coast is a 57km stretch of coast in Queensland, Australia, between Brisbane (80km north on the M1) and the NSW border. Burleigh Heads sits in the middle of the southern half — 22km north of Gold Coast Airport at Coolangatta, 14km south of Surfers Paradise, and 26–32 minutes to all four major theme parks (Movie World, Sea World, Wet 'n' Wild, Dreamworld).

Free Parking — Why It Matters

On-site parking is free and included with every room. For context: paid parking in Surfers Paradise hotels runs $20–$35 per night. Street parking around Burleigh Heads is metered or time-limited in most of the beach-adjacent streets. The motel's dedicated guest parking takes this entirely off the table — you pull in, park, and it is done.

For tradespeople, families with cars full of gear, or anyone driving between theme parks, this matters quite a lot in practice. It is also common for budget accommodation comparisons to show a motel at $125 against a hotel at $170 — and to forget to add the $28 parking to the hotel figure. The total becomes $125 vs $198, which changes the comparison entirely.

Beach Access — 200 Metres, Flat 6-Minute Walk

The motel is directly on the Gold Coast Highway in Miami, with Miami Beach on the foreshore directly in front of the property. Burleigh Beach — the main patrolled surf beach — is 200m from the motel (a flat 6-minute walk). There is no highway to cross on foot; the foreshore path runs the full length between Miami and Burleigh.

Burleigh Heads National Park is 1.2km from the motel — a 15-minute walk along the beach. The national park walk itself is free, takes about 45 minutes to complete, and gives elevated cliff-top views back toward the Gold Coast skyline that are among the better views anywhere on the strip.
